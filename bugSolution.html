The solution is straightforward: escape the < and > symbols using their HTML entity equivalents, &lt; and &gt;, respectively. This tells the browser to treat these characters as literal text rather than HTML tags.

Here's an example:

<p>This is a paragraph with escaped less-than &lt; and greater-than &gt; symbols.</p>

By replacing the literal characters with their HTML entities, the browser will correctly render the text without attempting to parse them as tags.
